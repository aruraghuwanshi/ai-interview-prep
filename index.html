The 2026 divide: AI-off vs AI-on

The whole field split into two interview philosophies. You must train for both — but your targets (Anthropic & OpenAI) still run unaided live coding.

AI-OFF

Code by hand, live

Live rounds with AI disabled (CoderPad/CodeSignal "AI-off" mode). They test raw decomposition, debugging, and reasoning under observation. Recordings are audited afterward.

  • Why: once AI can answer on demand, they can't separate your skill from a tool's unless they turn it off.
  • Includes Anthropic & OpenAI live SWE coding rounds.
  • Train this: build the core problems from memory, no autocomplete.
AI-ON

Drive the AI, well

Take-homes and "AI-assisted" rounds where using AI is allowed or required. They grade how you prompt, verify, and integrate — production quality, tests, documented trade-offs.

  • Why: it mirrors the real job (most lab code now ships with AI help).
  • Includes OpenAI's paid 48h take-home; Anthropic's prep/take-home; Canva/Shopify live.
  • Train this: build real projects with AI, then defend every line.
Your working rule: our projects run AI-ON (that's the job). Before each interview, flip to AI-OFF "defend & extend" reps on the same problems so the live muscle is there.

Top companies: who makes you code by hand?

The real split in 2026 is live rounds (almost everyone allows AI in take-homes). Policies shift and vary by team/level — always confirm with your recruiter in writing.

AI-OFF

Code by hand in live rounds

AI disabled during live technical rounds; unaided reasoning is the signal. Post-hoc recording audits are common.

  • Anthropic your targetBans AI in live SWE assessments (auto-DQ). Encourages Claude for prep & take-homes; some ML/agent roles hand you a model.
  • OpenAI your targetLive coding unaided; 48h take-home allows AI with disclosure.
  • AmazonProhibited in coding rounds; violations flagged as do-not-hire.
  • AppleProhibited across technical rounds.
  • Google mostlyMost product teams AI-free; reverted to in-person after overlay-cheating. May-2026 pilot allows Gemini only in code-comprehension for some junior/mid roles.
  • MicrosoftMost teams prohibit; some AI-focused groups experimenting.
  • NetflixProhibited / discouraged in live.
  • StripeStrictest: no AI in live and take-homes — wants raw output + reasoning.
  • Quant (Jane Street, Citadel, HRT, Two Sigma)Unaided reasoning by design.
AI-ON

Allow / encourage AI in interviews

AI is welcomed or required, sometimes even in live rounds. They grade how you prompt, verify, and integrate.

  • Canva requiresSince Jun 2025, backend/frontend/ML candidates must use Cursor/Copilot/Claude in the technical screen.
  • Shopify"AI-first" — expects AI use; not using it reads as out-of-date.
  • Meta hybridOct-2025 pilot: AI-enabled CoderPad (GPT-5/Claude/Gemini/Llama). At E7+/M1 it replaces a coding round; optional below. Standard loops still AI-off.
  • Cursor / AnysphereAI-native; expects model use + agentic rounds, sometimes raw API.
  • Replit · Cognition (Devin) · LovableExplicitly allow/expect AI; often an agentic round.
  • MistralUses own/competitor models in-round; prompt-engineering + eval focus.
  • GitLab · ZapierAI allowed live per recruiter; Zapier gates on AI fluency for all new hires.
  • Notion · Rippling · VercelPermissive; AI-friendly (esp. take-homes).
  • Most YC / seed-stage startups"Use whatever tools you'd use on the job" — effectively AI-permitted.
Reading the table: the harder a company leans on AI as a product, the more it expects you to use AI well — except in the one live round it keeps AI-off to measure raw reasoning. Anthropic & OpenAI are the clearest example of that split, which is exactly why this plan trains both modes.
